Hi! Welcome to my PhD research website.

I study birds, seasonal environments, and gut microbiome communities.

The goal of my PhD study is to address the impact of seasonal environmental variation, and the global variation therein, on the gut microbiome community composition and function in birds.

I explore seasonal changes in the gut microbiome communities of birds from the perspective of physiological and immune function, using an environment–bird–microbiome model system.

This website is a personal space to record my PhD research, project development, academic notes, and related reflections.

Research

Urban pigeons

This project focuses on seasonal variation in urban feral pigeons in Groningen, with an interest in how diet, environment, and microbial communities may change across seasons and locations.

Skylarks

This project explores temporal variation in wild skylarks across months, years, and annual cycle stages.

Short-term changes in wild birds

This project examines short-term changes in wild birds and how these changes may relate to ecological and physiological conditions.
